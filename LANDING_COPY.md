## Headline Iterations (rejected)

| Version | Headline | Why Rejected |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| v1 | "Audit Your AI Spend in 3 Minutes" | Too feature-focused, not benefit-focused |
| v2 | "Your Team Is Wasting Money on AI Tools" | Too accusatory, creates defensiveness |
| v3 | "Stop Overspending on AI Tools" | ✅ Direct, benefit-first, action-oriented |
| v4 (tested) | "Find Out How Much Your AI Stack Is Costing You" | Too passive, no urgency |

**Why v3 won:** "Stop" is an action word that implies the user has agency. "Overspending" is specific — it implies there's a right amount to spend, and they're above it. No jargon.

## FAQ Copy

**Q: Is this really free?**
Yes. The audit is completely free. No credit card, no account required. You get the full report instantly.

**Q: How does the audit engine work?**
We compare your reported spend against official benchmark pricing for each tool and plan. Our rule engine detects overprovisioned plans, duplicate tools, and team-size mismatches — then calculates exact monthly and annual savings.

**Q: Which AI tools do you support?**
We currently support Cursor, ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, Gemini, OpenAI API, Anthropic API, Windsurf, v0, Midjourney, Perplexity, Notion, Linear, Vercel, and GitHub — with more being added regularly.

**Q: Is my data stored?**
Audit results are stored anonymously to generate your shareable report link. We never sell your data. If you submit your email for the report, it is stored securely and used only to send your audit.

**Q: What if I don't know my exact spend?**
Use your best estimate. The audit engine compares your input against official plan pricing, so even approximate numbers will surface meaningful savings opportunities.

## Copy Principles

1. **Dollar figures over percentages** — "$684/month" is more visceral than "38% savings"
2. **Specific over vague** — "Cursor Business → Pro saves $40/seat" beats "consider downgrading"
3. **Action-oriented** — every CTA is a verb ("Start", "Run", "Email")
4. **No jargon** — "AI tools" not "LLM infrastructure", "save money" not "optimize spend"
5. **Honest about limitations** — "use your best estimate" acknowledges imperfect data rather than pretending the tool is magic
